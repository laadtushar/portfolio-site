POV: You work 16 hours a day while all your friends enjoy their 20s

Created on 2022-09-25 18:17

Published on 2022-09-25 19:15

I won't lie; I am incredibly jealous of all my classmates who are enjoying their lives, partying, or taking trips & vacations. Those Instagram stories that pop up every day are frustrating.

What did I do not to deserve that fun? Am I losing my prime years to work? Why can't I go out clubbing every day? These questions cloud my mind at the end of a productive day at work. I have talked to a few trusted people about this, and they all agree that I should have a life. I am 20, and I am overworking. But I dismiss them by saying, "It's my hustle to do, and it will all be worth it one day."

"It will all be worth it one day" that's what I keep telling myself, but I would be lying if I said I believe in that shit. I don't know the future; maybe I will regret not enjoying my early 20s; maybe at the end of the day we all will be standing on the same boat, and I wouldn't have achieved anything extra.

In order to understand why I have a 16-hour work life, Let me give you a quick insight into a day in my life. I wake up at 6:15 AM, get ready, and reach college by 7:15 AM. I sign into the office (remote work) at 7:30 AM and work until my first lecture; after that, I keep switching between work and college until 6:30 PM, after which I leave for home and reach by 7:00 PM. I haven't signed out of the office yet because I still have a lot of work and have not completed my daily working hours. So I work until 8:30 or 9:00 PM, after which I have dinner. Before I sleep, I finish some work for clients I have taken up in my startup (Build My Site). After this, I go to bed by 10:30 PM. The last thing I do at night is, scroll some reels on Instagram, listen to some songs and feel sad about my pathetic social life.

You would ask what keeps me going and what's stopping me from giving up on everything and just sleeping for a week at least.

Let me tell you a story; I choose weekends to fix my sleep schedule by continuously sleeping for 16-18 hours. The other weekend I was asleep for the last 12 hours during the day, and I was about to wake up when I heard my father ask my mother, "Why is he sleeping during the day?" My mother said, "Let him sleep; he works hard the whole week." There's your answer. My mom knows I work hard, and that's all I need to keep going.

My mother is the earning member of the family - I have seen her take up enormous responsibilities that any Indian woman needs to take up while also earning for the whole family and getting food on the table. I am probably just trying to be like her, and maybe one day, I will be able to give her the life of comfort that she has always ensured for me.

So apart from the fact that I was bullied my whole childhood and I have a pathological need to prove to others that I am better than them and that you can't bully me anymore, It's the urge to make my mother proud that helps me keep the hustle up.
